My Analytics Projects

In this page you will find my various Data Analytics / HR Analytics projects where I demonstrate my expertise in Excel, Tableau, SPSS, Statistics, Critical Thinking, and Problem Solving.

 

At the bottom of the page you will also find links to my Resume and my Github, where you can view all the code that I wrote to create this website.

 

Tableau Dashboard: CitiBike Ride Data Visualizations for February 2018

This Tableau dashboard demonstrates various key visualizations on Manhattan Citi Bikes rides during the month of February 2018. Over 800-thousand rides were made that month, and my dashboard shows the most popular start stations, most popular end stations, most common riding times, and more. I can share the raw data with you upon request.

 

Tableau Dashboard: Heatmap & Treemap of Stock Returns on May 22, 2021

This Tableau dashboard demonstrates a heatmap+treemap of stock returns on May 22, 2021. My dashboard shows the performance of 12 stocks on that day, including stock price percent changes for the day plus the market cap for each stock. The data was pulled using live data from Google Finance, which was then imported to Tableau.

 

Real-World Consulting Project: Management Relations Survey Analysis

Real-world management survey and report conducted as part of my graduate program's Behavioral Consulting course. Employees from various companies and industries were given a Likert-scale survey to complete. The answers of 48 participants were analyzed using t-tests and ANOVA tests on SPSS. The results and conclusion can be found in the report.

 

HR Analytics Mock Case Study: Comparing the demographics of a company with demographics reports from the U.S. Census

HR Analytics report where I compare a mock company's employee demographics with the demographics of the 2019 U.S. population. This real-world was pulled from the U.S. Census Bureau website. Using Excel pivot tables, I compared the demographic percentages of the two data sets, and made recommendations on where the company could improve it's employee representations. The Excel report can be provided upon request.

 

Real-World Developmental Psychology Research Study: Outgroup Empathy in Young Children

This is the final report of a real-world, 3-semester long psychology study I conducted as an undergrad. I worked with a professor and two other students in researching, designing, and executing this developmental psychology experiment where we tested how children respond to outgroup exposure books. We Used SPSS to run various types of statistical analyses (t-tests, ANOVAs, regressions) on our data of 80+ participants and to create appropriate graphs and tables. We presented our findings at the 2017 Cognitive Development Society Conference, a national developmental psychology conference held in Portland, Oregon. The statistical analyses and results can be found in the report.
